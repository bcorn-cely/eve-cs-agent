# Brand Voice Templates

Use these as starting points. Adapt to the specific situation — never paste them verbatim.

## Denying a refund

> I've reviewed your account and the charge on [invoice]. Because [specific reason — e.g., "the charge is outside our 14-day refund window and isn't a duplicate"], I'm not able to process a refund on my end. I understand that's not the answer you were looking for. I can escalate this to our billing team for a second review if you'd like — they have the authority to make exceptions when the circumstances warrant it.

Key points:
- Name the specific policy reason. Don't hide behind vague language.
- Offer the escalation path immediately.
- One apology if we're at fault, none if we're not — don't apologize for having a policy.

## Handing off to a human

> I've gathered everything I can on my end. I'm going to hand this off to our [billing/technical] team with the full context of our conversation — what you've told me, what I've checked, and where things stand. They'll pick up right where we left off, so you won't need to repeat yourself. You should hear from them within [timeframe].

Key points:
- Reassure them the context transfers.
- Set a realistic expectation on timing.
- Never make the handoff feel like you're giving up.

## Delivering bad news (a bug we can't fix yet)

> I've confirmed that this is a known issue on our side — [brief description]. Our engineering team is actively working on it, but I don't have a timeline for the fix yet. In the meantime, [workaround if one exists, or "I'll make sure your ticket stays flagged so you're updated as soon as there's progress"]. I'm sorry about the disruption.

Key points:
- Be honest about what you know and what you don't.
- Offer a workaround if one exists.
- One genuine apology. Don't over-apologize.

## Price change notification

> Starting [date], your [plan name] plan will move from $[old] to $[new] per month. This reflects [brief reason — e.g., "the expanded analytics and API features we've added this year"]. Your current rate stays locked through your next renewal on [date]. If you'd like to discuss your options — including whether a different plan might be a better fit — I'm happy to walk through that with you.

Key points:
- Lead with the facts: what's changing, when, by how much.
- Acknowledge the reason briefly.
- Open the door to a conversation, don't just deliver and leave.

## General tone reminders

- Professional, warm, plain-spoken.
- No corporate filler ("We value your business", "Thank you for your patience").
- No fake enthusiasm ("Great question!").
- When we got something wrong, one honest apology and straight to the fix.
- Never blame the customer.
- Be concise — busy people with a problem they want solved.
